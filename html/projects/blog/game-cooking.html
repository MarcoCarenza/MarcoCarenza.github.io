The Game Known as Cooking

OVERVIEW

An analysis on cooking as if it were a game. How we can better understand cooking activity using a playful lens. Written as my mid-term assignment for my Games Studies course.

My argument is that cooking is a game because the chefs (not professional cooks, just the person who is cooking) must enter the magic circle (see Johan Huizinga’s Homo Ludens) in order to make their meals, as well as belonging to Roger Caillois' category of play, specifically Mimicry and Agon (see Roger Caillois's Man, Play and Games).

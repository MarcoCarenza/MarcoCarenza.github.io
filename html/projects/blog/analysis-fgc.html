Fighting games and the Fighting Game Community (FGC): How their history led them to diverge from other competitive genres

OVERVIEW

An analysis on Fighting games and its community diverged from other competitive video game genres. Written as my final assignment for my Games Studies course, where I was fortunate enough to present it at our school conference.

With this paper I wanted to investigate what makes fighting games and its community so different from other competitive games and their communities. I will be diving into fighting games’ history and how the community evolved to what they are today.

Overall, my findings were that fighting games diverge from other genres due to their arcade origins, with the lack of serviceable online services leading the Fighting Game Community (FGC) to rely and develop their own offline spaces known as locals. Additionally, fighting games tend to be individual play compared to team-play like other genres, leading to the outcome of each match being directly tied to your performance.

Finally, they are a hard genre to get into, so those who get into the genre tend to stick with the genre.
